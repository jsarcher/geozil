ANALYTICS

Geographic Analytics on Zilliqa Node Distribution

The infrastructure of centralized web-services often is subject to single-point of failure, which can be a threat with adversarial entities in the system. Within permission-less distributed networks this risk is mitigated by nodes being operated within various geographic, cultural and political areas. Such a network is referred to be decentralized. A quantitative measurement of decentralization is crucial to benchmark the health of a decentralized network. Geographic localization of participating nodes is required in order to achieve comprehensive network health monitoring. There are many more measurements for drawing conclusions on a network's health, e.g. hash rate distribution. Incrementally enriching obtained data with additional information, e.g. average node latencies, advanced analytics can be applied.
